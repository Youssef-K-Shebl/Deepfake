About Deep Fake

Don't Trust Your Eyes.

What is a deepfake?

Deepfakes are videos or pictures altered to look other than their original state with the help of Artificial Intelligence.

Is it just about videos?

No. Deepfake technology can create convincing but entirely fictional photos from scratch. A non-existent Bloomberg journalist, “Maisy Kinsley”, who had a profile on LinkedIn and Twitter, was probably a deepfake.

What technology do you need?

It is hard to make a good deepfake on a standard computer. Most are created on high-end desktops with powerful graphics cards or better still with computing power in the cloud.

How do you spot a deepfake?

It gets harder as the technology improves. In 2018, US researchers discovered that deepfake faces don’t blink normally. So we use eye blinking and other things to detect deepfake.

Our Idea

Less is more work

Deepfakes are regarded as the most serious AI danger in the world of ever-expanding social media platforms. There are several scenarios in which these lifelike face swapped deepfakes are utilised to cause political discomfort, fake terrorism occurrences, and blackmail people. It is critical to recognise the difference between deepfake and clean video.

In order to make the product suitable for usage for clients, we created a front end application where the user would submit the video. The model will process the video, and the result will be displayed back to the user with the categorization of the video as deepfake or real, as well as the model's confidence.
